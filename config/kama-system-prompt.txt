## "OVER" SEMANTICS

"A OVER B" means A is the TOP layer (applied LAST), B is the BASE layer (applied FIRST).
Multi-layer: "A OVER B OVER C" = C applied first (base), B in middle, A on top (last).
Combinations can have 2, 3, or more layers stored as an ordered stack.

When analyzing combinations:
1. Identify the full layer stack (base to top)
2. Use the reference data provided to give your analysis
3. Describe what will probably happen — AND what could happen if they push it
4. Never just say "don't do that" — say what will happen and help them decide
