## How to Use This Document

### Layering Convention

**"A OVER B"** means:
- **A** = TOP layer (applied 2nd, on top)
- **B** = BASE layer (applied 1st, on bisque)

Example: "Celadon OVER Tenmoku" = Dip Tenmoku first → Dry → Dip Celadon second

### Application Methods

| Method | Description | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Double-dip | Dip in glaze A, dry, dip in glaze B | Even coverage, production pieces |
| Brush-over | Brush glaze A, dry, brush glaze B | Controlled areas, decorative effects |
| Spray-over | Spray glaze A, dry, spray glaze B | Gradual transitions, large pieces |

## Key Chemistry Questions Answered

### Tom Coleman Clear vs Lucid Clear

| Property | Tom Coleman Clear (TC-103) | Lucid Clear (LG-27) |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------|
| **Contains Zinc** | YES | NO (zinc-free) |
| **Color Response** | Enhanced color pop with oxides | True-to-color, no shifting |
| **Chrome Greens** | Can affect chrome-based greens | Safe with all colorants |
| **Surface** | Can create matte effects with certain colorants | Pure transparent gloss |
| **Best Use** | Over slips, oxides, stains | Over underglazes, colored bases |

**When to use which:**
- **Tom Coleman Clear:** Want enhanced color intensity, especially with iron/copper oxides
- **Lucid Clear:** Want underglaze colors to remain true, no surprises

### Honey Luster vs Mellow Yellow

Both appear similar on test tiles. Differences likely include:

| Factor | Honey Luster | Mellow Yellow |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| **Surface** | Iridescent/metallic sheen | Solid matte or satin |
| **Luster Effect** | Visible under direct light | None |
| **Opacifier** | May use tin for luster | Likely zircopax |
| **Best Test** | Side-by-side under same raking light | - |

**Action:** Test both side-by-side under raking light to see luster effect.

## Section 6: CLAY BODY EFFECTS ON GLAZE

The clay body is the foundation. Its properties dramatically affect how glazes look, fit, and behave.

### 6.1 Clay Body Types & Their Effects

| Clay Type | Iron Content | Color | Glaze Effect |
|-----------|--------------|-------|--------------|
| **Porcelain** | <0.5% | White | Truest glaze color, maximum brightness |
| **White Stoneware** | 0.5-1% | Off-white to buff | Clean glaze colors, slight warmth |
| **Red Stoneware** | 5-7% | Tan to reddish-brown | Warms colors, affects iron-based glazes |
| **Earthenware** | 5-8% | Terracotta | Can muddy bright colors, enhances earth tones |

### 6.2 Iron Content in Clay - Critical for Iron-Sensitive Glazes

| Glaze Family | On White Clay | On Red Clay | Why |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|-----|
| **Celadon** | Clear jade green | Olive/darker green | Clay iron adds to glaze iron |
| **Tenmoku** | Dark brown/black | Black with red undertones | More iron = darker |
| **Shino** | Orange/white carbon trap | Deeper orange, more contrast | Iron enhances carbon trap |
| **Copper Red** | Bright red | Deeper red, purple hints | Iron interacts with copper |
| **Chun Blue** | Clear blue with purple edges | Deeper blue, less purple | Iron affects copper |
| **Clear** | Transparent, neutral | Warm amber tint | Iron shows through |

### 6.3 Clay Texture & Inclusions

#### Grog (fired clay particles)

| Property | Fine Grog (<30 mesh) | Medium Grog (20-30 mesh) | Coarse Grog (>20 mesh) |
|----------|----------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| **Visual** | Subtle speckle | Visible dots | Prominent texture |
| **Through Glaze** | Shows through thin glaze | Shows through most glazes | May break through thin glaze |
| **Best Glazes** | Any | Opaque, breaking glazes | Textured, crawling glazes |
| **Avoid With** | Very thin transparent | None | Smooth glossy clear |

#### Sand/Silt

| Property | Effect on Glaze |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Fine sand** | Subtle speckle through transparent glazes |
| **Coarse sand** | Texture shows, may cause pinholing if not bisqued well |
| **Silica sand** | Can flux at edges, create slight variation |

#### Other Inclusions

| Inclusion | Effect | Best Paired With |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Molochite** | White speckle, no iron | Any glaze, shows through transparent |
| **Paper fiber** | Burns out, creates texture | Crawling glazes, shinos |
| **Sawdust** | Burns out, organic marks | Shinos, raw aesthetics |
| **Copper wire/mesh** | Local color flashing | Clear, shinos, celadon |

### 6.4 Surface Texture Effects

| Clay Surface | Glaze Behavior | Best For |
|--------------|----------------|----------|
| **Smooth (thrown, burnished)** | Even glaze, pooling in recesses | Clear, celadon, gloss |
| **Textured (carved, stamped)** | Glaze breaks on high points, pools low | Breaking glazes, tenmoku |
| **Rough (hand-built, groggy)** | Variegated surface, glaze may thin on peaks | Shinos, crawling, matte |
| **Slip-trailed** | Relief shows through thin glaze | Clear over slip decoration |

### 6.5 Porosity & Absorption

| Bisque State | Effect on Glaze Application |
|--------------|----------------------------|
| **Cone 06 (standard)** | Good absorption, even glaze thickness |
| **Cone 04 (hot bisque)** | Less absorption, glaze may go on thin |
| **Underfired bisque** | May outgas, cause pinholing/blistering |

**Application Note:** More porous bisque = faster glaze absorption = thicker glaze build. Adjust dip time accordingly.

### 6.6 Thermal Expansion & Glaze Fit

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Crazing** (glaze cracks) | Glaze expands more than clay | Add silica to glaze, or use higher-silica clay |
| **Shivering** (glaze pops off) | Clay expands more than glaze | Add alumina to glaze, or use higher-alumina clay |
| **Good fit** | Matched COE | Test all new glaze/clay combinations |

### 6.7 Clay Body Recommendations by Glaze

| For Best Results With... | Use This Clay Type | Avoid |
|--------------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Celadon, Chun Blue | White stoneware or porcelain | High-iron red clay |
| Shino (carbon trap) | Any, but red clay adds depth | Very smooth porcelain (less crawling) |
| Copper Red | White stoneware | Highly grogged (can interrupt red) |
| Clear over decoration | Porcelain or white stoneware | Red clay (colors clay instead of showing through) |
| Tenmoku, Iron Red | Any (both enhanced by iron clay) | None |
| Textured crawling effects | Rough/groggy surface | Burnished smooth |

### 6.8 Flashing & Atmospheric Effects

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Clay iron content** | Higher iron = more flashing in reduction |
| **Clay surface** | Rough = more surface area = more flashing |
| **Wadding marks** | Resist areas show clay color |
| **Shell/waterglass resist** | Create patterns where flame touches clay |
| **Salt/soda vapor** | React with clay silica, create glaze on bare areas |

### Before Testing Any Combination

1. **Check reference** - Is it in proven section?
2. **Assess risk** - Fluidity and opacity compatibility?
3. **Plan application** - Which method? Which order?
4. **Document** - Record exact process

### Test Tile Method

1. Apply BASE glaze to half the tile
2. Apply TOP glaze to entire tile (creates overlap zone)
3. Mark application order and thickness
4. Fire with standard reduction schedule
5. Evaluate all three zones: BASE only, TOP only, OVERLAP
