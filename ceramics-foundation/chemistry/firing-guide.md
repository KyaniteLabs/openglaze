### Cone Accuracy
- A cone accurately measures temperature when bent to approximately **90 degrees**
- For self-supporting cones: tip touching the base
- Use witness cones in multiple kiln levels to verify temperature distribution

### Cone Packs
- **Composition:** Guide cone (lower than target), firing cone (target), guard cone (higher than target)
- **Placement:** Visible through peepholes, typically at multiple kiln levels
- **High-fire use:** For cone 10 reduction, use lower cones (011, 010, 09, 4) to determine when to start reduction, and higher cones (6, 8, 9, 10) for glaze maturity

## Reduction vs Oxidation Firing

### Oxidation Firing

**Definition:** Firing technique where oxygen is freely available to interact with clay and glazes.

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Atmosphere** | Oxidizing - complete combustion with sufficient oxygen |
| **Flame** | Blue, short, and bushy |
| **Kiln Atmosphere** | Clear |
| **Sound** | Roaring |
| **Combustion** | Gas combusts at burner tips |

**Effects on Glazes:**
- Bright, saturated glaze colors
- Deep brick red clay bodies
- Complete carbon burn-out from raw clay
- White glazes stay pure white
- Intensified primary and secondary colors
- Muted earth tones

**Implementation:**
- **Electric kiln:** Simple - no steps needed to limit airflow
- **Gas kiln:** Vents adjusted to allow enough oxygen inside
- **Airflow:** Circulates freely through kiln at high heat

### Reduction Firing

**Definition:** Kiln firing technique where oxygen is partially restricted, creating a reducing atmosphere that affects clay and glaze chemistry.

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Atmosphere** | Reducing - insufficient oxygen for complete combustion, creates carbon monoxide |
| **Flame** | Lengthy, gentle orange or yellow |
| **Kiln Atmosphere** | Murky |
| **Peepholes** | Orange or yellow flames at damper and peepholes |
| **Black Smoke** | Indicates gas overstock (too much reduction) |

**Effects on Glazes:**
- Muted and grayed glaze colors
- Darkened clay bodies
- Increased metallic luster in glazes
- Reduction speckling and flashing
- Rich dark browns and blacks
- Metallic luster in copper and iron glazes
- Carbon trapping in white glazes

**Chemistry:** Oxygen deprivation causes elements to gain electrons as they give up oxygen, altering energy levels and physical structure.

**Implementation:**
- **Gas kiln (primary):** Partially close damper, decrease primary air, or increase gas pressure
- **Electric kiln (limited):** Pack pots with carbon materials (sawdust, paper), spray carbonate solution, add reducing agents to kiln wash
- **Control Methods:**
  - Partially closing the damper
  - Increasing fuel supply
  - Introducing combustible materials (wood, oil)
- **Monitoring:** Observe flame color/behavior - more orange/smoky = stronger reduction

### When Reduction Starts

| Stage | Temperature Range | Purpose |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| **Typical Start** | Cone 012 to Cone 010 (~858-898°C / 1576-1648°F) | |
| **Body Reduction** | Cone 012-08 | Form reds in clay body |
| **Glaze Reduction** | Continue through to end | Develop glaze effects |
| **Carbon Trap Shinos** | ~850°C (1562°F) | When soda begins to melt |
| **Final Oxidation** | Cone 9 to Cone 10 (final 15-30 min) | Smoother, brighter glaze surfaces (optional) |

**Important:** Starting reduction after cone 06 may miss effects. Reduction takes 20-30 minutes around cone 010.

### Neutral Atmosphere
- **Definition:** Conditions between oxidation and reduction
- **Characteristics:** Greenish flames
- **Timing:** Typically maintained after reduction until reaching cone 9, then oxidation introduced

## Firing Schedules

### Bisque Firing (Cone 06)

**Purpose:** Create porous yet sturdy form for glazing, burn out organic materials

**Critical Temperatures:**
- **100°C / 212°F:** Boiling point - water must evaporate slowly
- **573°C / 1063°F:** Quartz inversion - structural change in clay, causes stress
- **400-700°C:** Carbon burnout - organic materials burn out

**Schedule:**

| Step | Rate (°F/hr) | Rate (°C/hr) | Target (°F) | Target (°C) | Hold | Purpose |
|------|--------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|------|---------|
| 1 | 80 | 44 | 250 | 121 | 0 | Gentle initial heating - remove residual moisture |
| 2 | 200 | 111 | 1000 | 538 | 0 | Ramp through water smoking and early decomposition |
| 3 | 100 | 56 | 1100 | 593 | 0 | Slow through quartz inversion (1060°F / 573°C) |
| 4 | 180 | 100 | 1695 | 924 | 0 | Continue to approach bisque temperature |
| 5 | 80 | 44 | 1832 | 1000 | 0 | Final approach to cone 06 |

**Estimated Time:** ~12-14 hours for tightly packed kiln

### Glaze Firing (Cone 10 Reduction)

**Purpose:** Mature high-fire glazes in reduction atmosphere

**Atmosphere Schedule:**

| Phase | Range | Purpose | Characteristics |
|-------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Initial Oxidation** | Room temp to Cone 010 (~898°C / 1648°F) | Prepare work, complete early oxidation | Blue flame, clear atmosphere |
| **Reduction Phase** | Cone 010 to Cone 9 (~898-1257°C / 1648-2295°F) | Create reduction effects in body and glazes | Orange/yellow flames, murky atmosphere |
| **Final Oxidation** | Cone 9 to Cone 10 (final 20 min) | Smoother, brighter glaze surfaces | Return to oxidizing (optional) |

**Temperature Schedule (Gas Kiln):**

| Step | Rate (°F/hr) | Rate (°C/hr) | Target (°F) | Target (°C) | Hold | Purpose |
|------|--------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|------|---------|
| 1 | 150 | 83 | 250 | 121 | 0 | Gentle heating, prevent thermal shock |
| 2 | 350 | 194 | 2050 | 1121 | 0 | Ramp through quartz inversion and early stages |
| 3 | 120 | 66 | 2250 | 1232 | 0 | Slower approach to peak temperature |
| 4 | 60 | 33 | 2290 | 1254 | 0 | Very slow approach to final temperature |
| 5 | 40 | 22 | 2315 | 1268 | 15 | Peak temperature hold for glaze maturity |
| 6 | 400 | 222 | 1700 | 926 | 90 | Controlled cooling for crystal development and stress relief |

**Estimated Time:** 12-16 hours depending on kiln size and loading

**Cooling Considerations:**
- **Quartz Inversion:** Avoid rapid cooling around 573°C / 1063°F
- **Cristobalite Conversion:** Avoid rapid cooling around 225°C / 437°F
- **Slow Cool Benefits:** For crystal-surface glazes, helps smooth bubbles
- **Paper Test:** Use paper through spy hole - when paper stops igniting (~230°C / 446°F), kiln is cool enough to open

## Kiln Controller Programming

### Basic Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ramp** | Controlled increase or decrease in temperature over specified period |
| **Hold** | Duration to maintain target temperature before proceeding |
| **Segment** | One ramp + hold combination |
| **Rate of Change** | Speed of temperature increase/decrease (°F/hr or °C/hr) |
| **Target Temperature** | Final temperature to reach during segment |

### Programming Modes

#### Ramp/Hold Mode
- Operator designs custom firing programs with 1 or more segments
- Complete control over rate, target, and hold for each segment
- **Uses:** Custom schedules, specific glaze requirements, controlled cooling, experimental firings

#### Cone Fire Mode
- Built-in programs that automatically adjust temperatures based on selected cone
- Select cone number and speed, controller handles the rest
- **Limitations:** Less customization, may not match specific glaze requirements

### Common Segment Patterns

| Segment Type | Rate (°F/hr) | Rate (°C/hr) | Target (°F) | Target (°C) | Purpose |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Initial Drying** | 80-150 | 44-83 | 200-250 | 93-121 | Remove residual moisture |
| **Water Smoking** | 200-300 | 111-167 | 1000-1100 | 538-593 | Drive off chemically combined water |
| **Quartz Inversion Slow** | 100-150 | 56-83 | 1000-1200 | 538-649 | Prevent cracking |
| **Main Ramp** | 300-400 | 167-222 | - | - | Efficient heating |
| **Final Approach** | 40-120 | 22-66 | - | - | Even heat distribution |
| **Peak Hold** | - | - | - | - | 10-30 min for glaze maturity |
| **Controlled Cool** | 200-500 | 111-278 | - | - | Prevent thermal shock |

### Temperature Conversions

**Target Temperatures:**
```
°F to °C: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9
°C to °F: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32
```

**Rate of Change:**
```
°F/hr to °C/hr: °C/hour = °F/hour × 5/9
°C/hr to °F/hr: °F/hour = °C/hour × 9/5
```

**Example:**
- Fahrenheit: Rate 150, Target 250
- Celsius: Rate 83, Target 121

### Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Possible Causes | Solutions |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Firing too slow** | Elements aging, lid not sealing, voltage drop, overloading | Check elements with ohm meter, replace if needed, check lid seal, reduce load |
| **Firing too fast** | New elements, higher voltage, light load | Adjust with slower ramps, use hold times |
| **Temperature gradient** | Hot and cold spots | Use witness cones, adjust loading, use hold times, check furniture arrangement |
| **Pinholes** | - | Longer hold at peak or slower bisque |
| **Crawling** | - | Check glaze thickness, application, fit |
| **Crazing** | - | Adjust glaze composition or cooling |
| **Shivering** | - | Different thermal expansion needed |

## Kiln Atmosphere Monitoring

### Oxidation Indicators
- Blue flames
- Clear atmosphere
- Roaring sound
- No back pressure

### Reduction Indicators
- Orange/yellow flames
- Murky atmosphere
- Back pressure at peepholes
- Black smoke (too much reduction)

### Neutral Indicators
- Greenish flames

## Heatwork Concept

**Pyrometric cones measure "heat work"** - the combination of time and temperature. A digital pyrometer gives accurate temperature, but cones indicate how the firing actually affected the ceramic ware.

- Slower ramps = lower temperature needed for same cone
- Faster ramps = higher temperature needed for same cone
- Always trust witness cones over pyrometer readings
